WHAT DO WE DO?

In the last 6 decades, humankind has been responsible for the mass production of plastic. This is accelerating and rapidly growing to a whopping 8.3 billion metric tons, and of course, most of that is in disposable products that cannot be recycled.

400 years to degrade

According to National Geographic, 91% of plastic is still not recycled. Plastic is one of those materials that take over 400 years to degrade, which means most of it still exists in some form. Even though only 12% of this plastic has been incinerated, this process is still responsible for the emission of toxic gases, which harm the planet's atmosphere.

ACQUISITION

Unfortunately, we live in a world where governments and powerful international organizations do not take drastic environmental action, so it's up to each of us to do whatever it takes to preserve not only the planet but life on the planet as well.
